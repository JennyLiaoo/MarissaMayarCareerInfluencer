Marissa Mayer

Who is Marissa Mayer and how did her contributions affect computer science?



Marissa Mayer's life prior to her fame

Marissa Mayer was born in 1975 in a town called Wausau, located in Wisconsin.
She lived with her father, mother, and brother. Her father worked as an environmental
engineer; her mother was an art teacher.

As she was choosing a college, Mayer applied to 10 schools. These schools included Harvard,
Yale, and Stanford. She got accepted to all the schools she applied for but ended up going
to Stanford. At first, she took pre-med courses. But her perspective on life changed when she took
an introductory computer science class. She then studied symbolic systems and computer science.
Receiving a B.S. degree in 1997 and a M.S degree the year she graduated. When she graduated
in 1999, she already had 12 job offers. Surprisingly, Mayer declined a high paying consulting job,
instead, she chose one that she estimated only had a 2% chance of surviving.




Working as Google's 20th employee

One of her job offers came from Google, at the time, a small start-up with less than 20 employees.
She wanted to see what the place would teach her. She took the offer from Google, becoming
their 20th employee. She worked there for the next 13 years of her life. Mayer started off at
Google, designing its home page, and creating its product management structure. She later
became Director of Consumer Web products. She oversaw the layout of Google’s well-known,
search homepage. When Google went public in 2004, Mayer became rich in an instant. During
her time there, she got credited for the increasing number of searches each day, making them
go from a few hundred thousand to more than a billion. She was also credited with the
success of Google’s search engine, as well as some Google products, like, Gmail, Google Maps,
and Google News. Unfortunately, in 2011, Mayer’s powerful run at Google came to a halt,
and ended.





Yahoo's CEO

In June of 2012, Mayer got another opportunity. Yahoo wanted Mayer to be its new CEO.
She got offered the job as a CEO and president. At the time, Yahoo was one of Google’s
biggest competitors. She accepted the offer and started working as Yahoo’s CEO. The problem
was, Yahoo’s revenue refused to pick up. She made a series of costly decisions that did not
go as planned. This led to her downfall. In July 2016, it was announced that Verizon was
officially buying Yahoo for almost 5 billion dollars. After this, Mayer resigned as the CEO.






After her resignation

Shortly after leaving Yahoo in 2017, Mayer co-founded Lumi Labs with her former Yahoo
colleague, Enrique Munoz Torres. Google’s onetime quarters are home to Lumi Labs, a
15-person start-up. Lumi Labs’ main purpose is to develop smartphone apps that help
people save a few minutes every day. For instance, machines learning certain photo-related
tasks. This can include figuring out if an image is blurry, if it is well lit, or whether it is one
that someone will be likely to share based on the history of photos that have been shared in
the past. This start-up is to focus “on building consumer applications enabled by artificial
intelligence.” Lumi Labs’ first project released in 2019, called Holiday Helper, is designed
to easily create mailing labels for gifts and cards. Mayer is pursuing new productivity-
enhancing technology.
